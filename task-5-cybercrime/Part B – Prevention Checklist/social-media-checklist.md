# Digital Literacy Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

---

## Section 1: General Online Safety
### Prevention Tip

1 Use strong passwords — mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers & symbols (e.g. R@ndom#72). Never use your name, DOB, or 'password123'.

2 Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all accounts — Gmail, Instagram, college portal — so even a stolen password won't compromise your account.

3 Only use trusted Wi-Fi networks — avoid logging into banking, email, or college portals on public Wi-Fi at cafes or bus stands. Use mobile data or a VPN instead.

4 Verify before you click — never click links in unknown SMS, WhatsApp messages, or emails. Check sender addresses carefully for subtle misspellings.

5 Keep your apps and phone updated — software updates patch security vulnerabilities. Enable auto-updates on your phone and laptop.

6 Download apps only from official stores — use Google Play Store or Apple App Store only. Avoid APK files from unknown websites.

---

## Section 2: Financial & UPI Safety (Critical)

The following tips are specifically relevant to UPI and digital payments used widely by students in India.

### Prevention Tip

7 Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone — no bank, government body, or company (including Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm support) will ever ask for your PIN or OTP. If someone asks, it is a scam.

8 Remember: Receiving money NEVER requires entering your PIN — a very common scam tricks students into entering their PIN to 'accept' money. Your UPI PIN is only needed when sending money.

9 Double-check the UPI ID and name before every transaction — always verify the recipient's name shown after entering their UPI ID. Scammers use IDs that look similar to real ones.

10 Avoid scanning QR codes sent by strangers — fake QR codes can silently initiate a payment from your account. Only scan QR codes at trusted physical shops or platforms.

## Section 3: Social Media & Identity Safety
### Prevention Tip

11 Do not overshare on social media — avoid posting your college name, hostel address, daily schedule, or ID card photos publicly. This info can be used for identity theft or targeted scams.12Be cautious of fake job/internship offers — scammers frequently target college students on WhatsApp and Telegram with fake work-from-home jobs asking for a 'registration fee'. Legitimate employers never ask for money upfront.
