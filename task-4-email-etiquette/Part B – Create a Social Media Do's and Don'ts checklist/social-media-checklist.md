Do's:

Set profiles to private and review privacy settings regularly
Think before posting — consider how it reflects on you professionally
Use platforms like LinkedIn to build a positive digital identity
Report misinformation, hate speech, and cyberbullying
Take screen breaks to maintain mental well-being

Don'ts:

Never share personal location or contact details publicly
Don't post unverified information or react impulsively online
Don't plagiarize or repost content without crediting the creator
Don't use weak or repeated passwords across platforms
Don't let social media interfere with academic focus
✅ Responsible Social Media Use — Do's and Don'ts for College Students
#	✅ DO's	 | ❌ DON'Ts
1	Do set your profiles to private and review your privacy settings regularly |	Don't share your location, address, phone number, or daily routine publicly
2	Do think before you post — ask yourself if you'd be comfortable with a professor or employer seeing it |	Don't post or share content in anger or without verifying it first
3	Do use social media to network professionally (LinkedIn, GitHub) and build a positive digital presence |	Don't plagiarize or repost others' work without giving proper credit
4	Do report fake news, hate speech, or cyberbullying using platform tools |	Don't engage with or spread misinformation, even if shared by friends
5	Do take regular breaks from social media to protect your mental health |	Don't let social media distract you during classes, study sessions, or exams
6	Do use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication on all accounts |	Don't use the same password across multiple platforms or share login credentials
7	Do be respectful and constructive in comments and discussions online |	Don't participate in trolling, cyberbullying, or spreading rumours about others
