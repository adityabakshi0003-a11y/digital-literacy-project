# ✅ Responsible Social Media Use — Do's and Don'ts for College Students
| | |
|---|---|
|	✅ DO's	 | ❌ DON'Ts |
| Do set your profiles to private and review your privacy settings regularly |	Don't share your location, address, phone number, or daily routine publicly |
| Do think before you post — ask yourself if you'd be comfortable with a professor or employer seeing it |	Don't post or share content in anger or without verifying it first |
| Do use social media to network professionally (LinkedIn, GitHub) and build a positive digital presence |	Don't plagiarize or repost others' work without giving proper credit |
| Do report fake news, hate speech, or cyberbullying using platform tools |	Don't engage with or spread misinformation, even if shared by friends |
| Do take regular breaks from social media to protect your mental health |	Don't let social media distract you during classes, study sessions, or exams |
| Do use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication on all accounts |	Don't use the same password across multiple platforms or share login credentials |
| Do be respectful and constructive in comments and discussions online |	Don't participate in trolling, cyberbullying, or spreading rumours about others |
